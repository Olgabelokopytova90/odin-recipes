Pasta Primavera

Description

Pasta primavera is a simple dish made with pasta and fresh vegetables. Many people think it’s an Italian dish but it’s actually an American dish which was created by chef Sirio Maccioni and two assisting chefs (the original used cream cheese).

It was first served at a restaurant in New York City then a recipe was published a few years later in New York Times helping bring the dish to fame

Ingredients

    In it, regular all-purpose flour yields chewy, bouncy noodles every time. Eggs – The key ingredient for adding richness and moisture to the dough! Olive oil – Along with the eggs, a splash of olive oil moistens the dough and helps it come together. Salt – Add it to the dough and the pasta water for the best flavor.

Steps

  1. Boil Water. Start with a very large pot of water, about 6 quarts per pound of pasta.
  2. Add Salt. Put in a lot of salt, about 3 tablespoons.
  3. Add the Pasta.
  4. Stir.
  5. Taste the Pasta.
  6. Drain.
  7. Removing Ravioli.
  8. Stir In the Sauce.
